Viktor E. Frankl, M.D., Ph.D.

The man who knew meaning.

Everything can be taken from a man but one thing: the last of the human freedoms—to choose one’s attitude in any given set of circumstances, to choose one’s own way.
Viktor Frankl

Psychiatrist and neurologist

Viktor E. Frankl was a neurologist when the holocaust changed his life forever. Frankl survived amid the death and suffering, taking note of what psychologies improved one's chances of survival. Frankl organized his ideas and experiences to form a new psychotherapy practice called logotherapy. Recognizing how important a sense of meaning is for coping successfully with the challenges of life, he believed focusing on one's atititudes, purposes, and goals was the key to a healthier mental life.

For more background, please visit: Viktor Frankl Institute & Wikipedia

Author and philosopher

Viktor E. Frankl wrote many books in his lifetime. His most famous book was Man's Search for Meaning, a bestseller first written in 1946 that is still read widely today. The book does an excellent job of outlining is thoughts on the human will to meaning.
